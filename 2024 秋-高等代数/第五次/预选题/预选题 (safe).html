  • 不选用的原因: 第一次测验的所有题目均考察过程. 没有判断填空之类的问题.

(1) Let v1,v2, and v3 be vectors in a certain linear space V. Suppose that the pairs (v1,v2), (v2,v3), and (v1,v3) are all linearly independent. Claim: (v1,v2,v3) is also linearly independent.

(2) Let U,V, and W be linear subspaces of a certain linear space. Claim: (U+V)∩W is a subspace of (U∩W)+(V∩W).

(3) Let U be a proper linear subspace of V. Claim: span(V∖U) may be a proper subspace of V.

(4) Let A and B be matrices of the same size. Claim:

r(A2)+r(B2)≥r(AB).

(5) Let M=(ABCD) be a block matrix such that

r(AC)=r(AB)=r(BD)=r(CD).

Claim: r(M)=r(AB).

(6) Let A be a real square matrix. Suppose that xTAx=0 for any vector x. Claim: A=O.

(7) Let F be a number field containing π. Claim: F=R.

(8) Claim: dim⁡N(AB)≤dim⁡(N(A))+dim⁡(N(B))​.

证明题 1

  • 不选用此题的原因: 第一周的作业解答出现过类似的证明. 属于 Strang 自称不会做的题目.

Let (v1,v2,…,vm) be distinct vectors in Rd such that

cos⁡θi,j=viT⋅vj∥vi∥⋅∥vj∥≡C

is a constant. Prove that mmax=d+1.

Since the construction of m=d+1 is well-known, it suffices to prove that m≤d+1.

Hint: You can assume that ∥vi∥≡1 for convenience. Set

Q:=(v1v2⋯vm)∈Rd×m.

Now consider the rank of QT⋅Q​.

Problem: (The decreasing sequence of rank) Let A∈Fn×n.

(0) r(Ap)<r(Aq) whenever N(Ap)⊊N(Aq). r(Ap)=r(Aq) whenever N(Ap)=N(Aq).

(1) Prove that if r(A)=r(A2), then r(A)=r(A3).

(2) Prove that if r(Ak)=r(Ak+1), then r(Ak)=r(Ak+t) for all t∈N.

(3) Give an example such that r(A)>r(A2)>⋯>r(A2024)=r(A2025).

(4) Prove that {r(Ak)−r(Ak+1)}k∈N is also decreasing, with a limit of 0​.

证明题 3

  • 不选用此题的原因: 为了让试卷少一题. (其实这题挺适合考试的).

Problem: (Birkhoff algorithm for bi-stochastic matrices)

Let F be a number field. All matrices are in F3×3. Let j be the column vector (1,1,1)∈F3.

(1) A matrix A is a permutation matrix if ai,j is either 0 or 1, and Aj=j=ATj. How many permutation matrices are there in F3×3?

(2) Is V={X∣Xj=XTj=j} a linear space? If not, find X0 such that {X−X0∣X∈V} is a linear space.

(3) Find the dimension of the linear space in (2).

(4) Prove that X∈V is always a linear combination of permutation matrices. Is such a combination unique?

(5) Prove that the sum of the coefficients in the linear combination from (4) equals 1​​.

奖励题 3

  • 不选用此题的原因: 取消奖励题. 这题适合作为学完三角矩阵的特征值后的巩固题.

Let F be a number field. Mn(F) is the linear space of all n×n matrices over F.

(1) The trace is a linear map defined as

tr:Mn(F)→F,X↦∑i=1nxi,i.

Show that V={Y∈Mn(F)∣tr(Y)=0} is a linear subspace of Mn(F).

(2) Find dim⁡V and provide a basis for V.

(3) Prove that for any X∈Mn(F), there exist finitely many

A1,A2,…,Am,B1,B2,…,Bm∈Mn(F)

such that X=∑i=1m(AiBi−BiAi).

(4) (Bonus) Prove that tr(A)=0 if and only if there exist B,C∈Mn(F) such that A=BC−CB. You can prove this with the following steps:

(4.1) Let Λ be the diagonal part of A. Prove that there exists a permutation matrix P and a diagonal matrix D such that

  • Λ=P−1DP−D,

  • D has distinct entries on its diagonal.

(4.2) Consider A=L−U, where U is upper triangular and L−D is strictly lower triangular. Show that L,D, and U have the same eigenvalues (counting multiplicities). Hence, there exist invertible R and S such that

R−1LR=S−1US=D.

(4.3) A=L−U=(RS−1)⋅(SR−1L)−(SR−1L)⋅(RS−1).

(4.4) This shows that for any sufficiently large field F,

{X∈Mn(F)∣tr(X)=0}={AB−BA∣A,B∈Mn(F)}.

Can you prove the existence of counterexamples over finite fields?
